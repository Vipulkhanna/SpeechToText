Here are a few key facts about Mahatma Gandhi. Gandhi developed satyagraha, a method of nonviolent resistance used to fight against injustice. His birthday, second October, is celebrated globally as the International Day of Nonviolence. Born in Porbandar, Gujarat, he married Kasurba Gandhi in 1883 at the age of 13 in a arranged marriage. Before leading India, he spent twenty one years in South Africa fighting for the rights of Indians, where he first employed civil disobedience after experiencing racial discrimination. He was a strict vegetarian, a proponent of Swadeshi, and was known for walking long distances, sometimes up to 10 miles a day, which he believed kept him healthy. While born Mohandas, he was commonly called Mahatma and Bapu. His smiling face on Indian rupee notes is taken from a 1946 photograph of him with British politician Lord Pathik Lawrence. His methods inspired major civil rights movements worldwide, including those led by Martin Luther King Junior and Nelson Mandela. Thank you.
