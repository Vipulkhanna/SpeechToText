# MEETING NOTES

## Summary
Mahatma Gandhi developed satyagraha, a method of nonviolent resistance, and his October 2nd birthday is celebrated as the International Day of Nonviolence. He spent 21 years in South Africa fighting for Indian rights, where he first employed civil disobedience, and was known for his vegetarianism, advocacy for Swadeshi, and long walks. Gandhi, also known as Mohandas, Mahatma, and Bapu, inspired major civil rights movements worldwide, including those led by Martin Luther King Jr. and Nelson Mandela.

## Chapters

### [00:00] Key Facts About Mahatma Gandhi

- Developed Satyagraha, a method of nonviolent resistance.
- His birthday, October 2nd, is celebrated as the International Day of Nonviolence.
- Born in Porbandar, Gujarat, and married Kasurba Gandhi in 1883 at age 13.
- Spent 21 years in South Africa fighting for Indian rights and first employed civil disobedience there.
- Was a strict vegetarian, a proponent of Swadeshi, and known for walking long distances.
- Born Mohandas, commonly called Mahatma and Bapu.
- His image on Indian rupee notes is from a 1946 photograph with Lord Pathik Lawrence.
- His methods inspired major civil rights movements worldwide, including those led by Martin Luther King Jr. and Nelson Mandela.
